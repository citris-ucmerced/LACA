DATA-ENABLED TRADING NETWORKS TO SUSTAIN GROUNDWATER-DEPENDENT AGROSYSTEMS IN THE SEMI-ARID WEST

Ag-Food-Tech research-outreach-education program proposal to the USDA National Institute of Food and Agriculture Agriculture and Food Research Initiative: Sustainable Agricultural Systems

PROBLEM DEFINITION

Groundwater-dependent agrosystems in the Western US are under constraint from changes to their primary input: water. New regulations, changing patterns, increasing prices and competition is leading to shifts in supply and demand management strategies among growers and water managers (e.g. canal companies, groundwater sustainability agencies, irrigation districts). Fallowing of American cropland due to lack of available water resources at the necessary times poses significant risks to not only our food, fiber and fuel production, but also rural communities dependent upon agricultural employment. Opportunities to trade water resources are hampered by a lack of data, physical infrastructure, security of transactions, and institutional design. Rural communities dependent on agrosystems can benefit from increased injections of jobs and funding from agtech industry.
